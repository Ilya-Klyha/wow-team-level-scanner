Definition of Ready (DOR) - Company Standard
Source: https://adgear.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/AGILE/pages/21735179128

Purpose:
The Definition of Ready (DoR) is a team-owned policy that determines whether a backlog item is
sufficiently prepared to begin work. It ensures the team has the necessary clarity and context to start work with confidence.

At its core, the DoR answers a simple question: Can we start this work with confidence?

How It Works:
The DoR functions as a gate that backlog items must pass through before being accepted into a sprint or actively worked on. It typically takes the form of a set of criteria that confirms a work item is well-formed and well-understood. The goal is to increase clarity and reduce risk before work begins. Common readiness criteria may include:
- Clearly defined acceptance criteria
- Dependencies identified and addressed
- Scope and priority established

See INVEST criteria for more information on how to use the technique on story readiness.

When to Use It:
- During backlog refinement, assess whether a story is ready for planning
- At sprint planning, when selecting work for commitment

Benefits:
Applying the Definition of Ready helps the team prevent false starts, reduce rework, and improve delivery predictability.
- Ensures work is scoped, understood, and feasible
- Builds shared ownership and accountability before work begins
- Avoids bringing ambiguous or risky items into the sprint
- Strengthens collaboration between product and engineering

Cautions or Pitfalls:
A poorly applied DoR can block progress or invite risk:
- Under-defined: Vague or missing DoRs allow unclear or risky work into sprints
- Over-rigorous: Excessive readiness criteria can stall valuable work or prevent experimentation
- Ignored: Skipping DoR leads to unclear scope, delivery risk, and team frustration
