DEFINITION OF READY (DoR) - OFFERING/EPIC
================================================================================

The "Definition of Ready" ensures that a software development ticket is
sufficiently prepared and understood by the development team before it is
pulled into a sprint or iteration. A ticket meeting the DoR is ready for
immediate development without requiring further clarification or investigation.

Criterion: User Stories/Requirements Clarity and Acceptance Criteria Defined
Description: Requirements are clearly articulated, concise, and understandable
to all team members. Specific, testable acceptance criteria are provided for
each Offering/Epic, outlining what constitutes successful completion.
Comments: Team during Program Demo should show and highlight that all
acceptance criteria have been met.

Criterion: Monitoring and Alerting Planned
Description: Necessary monitoring and alerting for the new feature or change
have been discussed and according work is being accounted for delivery of the
feature.
Comments: Configuration: what and where should be configured? Metrics: how do
we measure offering/task?

Criterion: Dependencies and Risks Identified
Description: All known technical or external dependencies are identified and
addressed, or a clear plan is in place to manage them. Risks associated with
the Offering/Epic are documented upfront.
Comments: Does the Team Have the Skills and Capabilities to Perform the Entire
Job? If not, what kind of help is needed. Support from other teams should be
already managed by Architects/Engineering Managers/Product Managers.

Criterion: Estimates Provided
Description: The development team has provided T-SHIRT size (estimate) for the
work, or the ticket has been broken down to a size that allows for reasonable
estimation.
Comments: FYI. T-shirts estimations are not written in the stone. It is
estimation based on the current knowledge of those who are estimating. In case
of significant difference between estimations on program and team level, there
is communication action required to be taken by PO or Team Representative.
Transparent and proactive communication with stakeholders is essential to build
trust, align expectations, and ensure successful project outcomes.

Criterion: Prioritization Confirmed
Description: The Offering/Epic's priority is agreed upon by the Product Owner
and the development team.

Criterion: Design/Technical Approach
Description: Initial design considerations or a high-level technical approach
are documented and approved by ARB, Privacy, and Security.
Comments: Spike/POC - this is task for team which can be used as input for
Offering/Epic. There are two ways to complete a PoC: the output value will be
a) useful or b) not useful

Working agreement:
- It is ok that PM/EM will come with Epic/Offering in advance to get early team
  feedback.
- Planned end date needs to be defined and communicated to the team, after work
  start on Epic/Offering.

DEFINITION OF READY (DoR) - STORY/TASK
================================================================================

The "Definition of Ready" ensures that a software development ticket is
sufficiently prepared and understood by the development team before it is
pulled into a sprint or iteration. A ticket meeting the DoR is ready for
immediate development without requiring further clarification or investigation.

Criterion: User Story/Requirement Clarity
Description: The user story or requirement is clearly articulated, concise, and
understandable to all team members.

Criterion: Acceptance Criteria and Measurement Defined
Description: Specific, testable acceptance criteria are provided for the user
story, outlining what constitutes successful completion.

Criterion: Monitoring and Alerting Configured (if applicable)
Description: Necessary monitoring and alerting for the new feature or change
have been configured.
Comments: Configuration: what and where should be configured? Metrics: how do
we measure story/task?

Criterion: Estimates Provided
Description: The development team has provided an estimate for the work, or the
ticket has been broken down to a size that allows for reasonable estimation.
Comments: Estimations are based on the whole team experience.
Reference: Story Estimation - Cheat Sheet - Google Sheets

Criterion: Dependencies
Description: Highlighted dependencies with other teams or milestones.
Comments: All blocking tasks are resolved.
