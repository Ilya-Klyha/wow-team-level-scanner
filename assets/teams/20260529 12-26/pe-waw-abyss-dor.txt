Definition of Ready (DoR) – STORY / TASK

The "Definition of Ready" ensures that a software development ticket is sufficiently prepared and understood by the development team before it is pulled into a sprint or iteration. A ticket meeting the DoR is ready for immediate development without requiring further clarification or investigation.

Criterion: User Story/Requirement Clarity
Description: The user story or requirement is clearly articulated, concise, and understandable to all team members.

Criterion: Acceptance Criteria and Measurement Defined
Description: Specific, testable acceptance criteria are provided for the user story, outlining what constitutes successful completion.

Criterion: Monitoring and Alerting Configured (if applicable)
Description: Necessary monitoring and alerting for the new feature or change have been configured.
Comments: Configuration: what and where should be configured? Metrics: how do we measure story/task?

Criterion: Estimates Provided
Description: The development team has provided an estimate for the work, or the ticket has been broken down to a size that allows for reasonable estimation.
Comments: Estimations are based on the whole team experience. Story Estimation - Cheat Sheet - Google Sheets

Criterion: Dependencies
Description: Highlighted dependencies with other teams or milestones.
Comments: All blocking tasks are resolved.
